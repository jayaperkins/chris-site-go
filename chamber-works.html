Winter Scenes: Movement 1 (Landscape)


Soprano, string quintet
Lyrics: Walter de la Mare
Language: English
Duration: 5:00

"Landscape” is the first movement in a yet-to-be-completed song cycle for soprano and string quintet. Each movement depicts a different experience of the winter season. In the opening movement, I wanted to capture the vastness of a frozen landscape and the solitary stillness of the text. I wrote this movement for performance by my sister, Chelsea Enloe, for her undergraduate senior vocal recital.

Text:

“Winter”
Clouded with snow
The cold winds blow,
And shrill on leafless bough
The robin with its burning breast
Alone sings now.

The rayless sun,
Day's journey done,
Sheds its last ebbing light
On fields in leagues of beauty spread
Unearthly white.

Thick draws the dark,
And spark by spark,
The frost-fires kindle, and soon
Over that sea of frozen foam
Floats the white moon.

- Walter de la Mare
